The commercial problem

More campaign activity does not guarantee more impact.

When message, production and delivery decisions are made separately, teams create more work—but not necessarily a stronger campaign.

The campaign gets busier while the reason to care gets harder to see.

01 / MESSAGE

No single reason to care.

The offer may be strong, but the audience cannot quickly understand why it matters or what makes it worth choosing.

02 / PRIORITIES

Too much gets made.

Assets are commissioned before the campaign has decided what deserves attention, so time and budget spread across low-value work.

03 / PRODUCTION

Budget follows habit.

Teams default to familiar formats and suppliers instead of choosing the production route the campaign actually needs.

Decide what matters before you decide what to make.

How delivery works

The campaign decides the production—not the other way round.

Finished production is not bundled into the retainer. That means the recommended route can serve the campaign rather than a predetermined production model.

Where needed, Campaign Producers can source, brief and oversee the right production team through a separately approved scope.

  • 01You bring: the offer, commercial objective, business evidence, constraints and decision-makers.
  • 02We lead: audience strategy, campaign direction, messaging, writing, production planning and creative judgement.
  • 03Your team and specialists deliver: the finished design, film, photography, animation, media, web and technical work.
  • 04Together, we review: what was produced, what the available evidence suggests and what should be prioritised next.

Before we begin

Clear answers about the model.

What is included in the monthly retainer?

Within the agreed scope and reserved capacity, the retainer includes audience and campaign strategy, concepts, messaging, copy, scripts, production planning, budget guidance and agreed creative direction and review.

Is finished production included?

No. Design, filming, photography, editing, animation, AI asset generation, web development, talent, music, media buying and third-party supplier costs are scoped and approved separately. Where needed, Campaign Producers can source, brief and oversee production through a separately approved scope.

Why is production separate?

It keeps the production recommendation independent. The campaign can use your internal team, your existing agency, specialist suppliers, AI production, traditional production or a combination—depending on what the work genuinely needs.

What does reserved monthly capacity mean?

We agree the highest-value priorities for each working period and use the retained time against them. The service is not an unlimited request queue. Additional campaigns, urgent work, extensive revisions and extra days are agreed separately.

Can you work with our in-house team or existing agency?

Yes. Campaign Producers can provide the campaign direction, writing, briefs and creative judgement while your existing team and specialists execute the finished work.

How much supplier management is included?

The Growth and Embedded partnerships include coordination and oversight at agreed decision points. Hands-on daily production project management, procurement and substantial additional revision rounds may require separate capacity.

Which partnership is right for us?

Choose Campaign Producer for one important campaign, Campaign Growth Partner for an ongoing programme with up to two active workstreams, and Embedded Campaign Producer when you need a campaign lead working regularly inside the business.

Do you guarantee campaign performance?

No responsible campaign partner can guarantee a commercial result. Performance also depends on the strength of the offer, production execution, media, customer journey, sales operation and market conditions. We improve the quality of the campaign decisions and use the available evidence to recommend what should happen next.
