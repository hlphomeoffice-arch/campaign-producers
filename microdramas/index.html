The dramatic mechanism

Every episode must earn the next.

Hook quickly. Make the central desire clear. Put something meaningful in the way. Escalate the conflict. End on a consequence the audience needs resolved.

  1. 01Hook

    Open on a promise the audience understands.

  2. 02Desire

    Make what the character wants unmistakable.

  3. 03Conflict

    Put a meaningful obstacle in the way.

  4. 04Escalate

    Increase pressure, consequence or emotional debt.

  5. 05Interrupt

    Cut where the answer becomes necessary.

  6. 06Reward

    Pay off attention, then open the next question.

The accumulated attention then moves towards the action the project exists to support: another episode, an enquiry, a purchase, a sign-up or the next step in a wider campaign.

Before you brief us

Useful answers.

What exactly is a microdrama?

A microdrama is a short, episodic story designed for mobile viewing, often in a vertical format. Its defining feature is not simply episode length. Each instalment uses a clear hook, compressed conflict and a continuation trigger that makes the next episode feel necessary.

Can you adapt my book or existing intellectual property?

Yes. We identify the source material's strongest audience promise, characters and repeatable conflicts, then reshape them for mobile-first episodes. Rights, approvals, tone and the elements that must remain faithful are agreed before development moves forward.

Can you create a pilot or a complete series?

Both. A pilot can test the concept, hook, characters and production route before a larger commitment. If the story and brief already justify a complete series, Campaign Producers can lead development, production planning, team assembly, oversight, post-production and delivery.

Do you use traditional production, AI or both?

We use traditional, AI-enabled and hybrid approaches. The recommendation depends on performance needs, visual world, rights, schedule, budget and delivery. AI is used where it improves the route, not as a default or a substitute for creative judgement.

How much does a project cost and how long does it take?

Cost and timing depend on development needs, episode count and length, cast, locations, visual complexity, production route, post-production and delivery requirements. After reviewing the brief, we recommend a scope, sequence and budget range. A pilot and a complete series are priced and scheduled differently.

How are rights handled?

In the vast majority of projects, full rights to the work are assigned to you. If a rare exception applies, we will explain and agree it with you before issuing a quote, so the rights position is clear from the outset.
